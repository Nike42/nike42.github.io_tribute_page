Nnamdi Benjamin Azikiwe

Father of Nigerian Nationalism

Political Career and Achievement

  • 1944: He co-founded the National Council of Nigeria and the Cameroons(NCNC) with Herbert Macaulay.

  • 1946: He became the secretary-general of the NCNC and was elected to the Legislative Council of Nigeria the following year.

  • 1960: He established the University of Nigeria, Nsukka, and Queen Elizabeth II appointed him to the Privy Council of the United Kingdom.

  • 1980: He was made Grand Commander of the Federal Republic (GCFR), Nigeria's highest national honour.

  • 1934: He devised a "Syllabus for African History" and wrote a book, Liberia in World Politics, in defense of the black republic
